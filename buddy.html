UX UI Project Case Study

Prescription Buddy is a self-initiated mobile UX/UI case study aimed at simplifying daily medication tracking—especially for older adults or those managing complex prescriptions. Designed over 4 weeks using Figma and Illustrator, the app guides users with visual pill logs, smart reminders, and a focus on clarity and accessibility. My role covered end-to-end design, from user flows to final UI.

Problem

  1. Failure: Patients often forget to take their medication in a correctly scheduled manner. This could result in medical complications, or make the medication unsuccessful.
  2. Abundance: With the rise in health advancements, patience tend to have multiple prescriptions to take. With this, patients can get confused or overwhelmed with large amounts to take.
  3. Confusion: Patients are frequently confused about their prescriptions and do not understand the names and meanings of their medications. This leaves patients scared and concerned.

Solution

  1. Friendly Reminders: Set reminders to encourage patients to intake their medicine at the correct time..
  2. Informational Setup: Patients can easily access information about their prescriptions in an easy and comprehensible way
  3. Concern Hot-line: Patients have the opportunity to send direct messages regarding their medications to their medical team.

1

Voice-enabled Reminders: To give the option for easier reminders, voice-enabled settings will allow users to set notifications easily and quickly.

2

Caregiver and Multi-User Management: Allowing account access to trusted sources can aid in the success of tracking and intake awareness.

3

Dark Mode Options: Users may experience eye strain due to the primary use of light colors. Dark mode options will widen usability.

4

Conduct Usability Testing with a Broader Audience: Expand testing to include users from varied age groups and medical backgrounds to uncover additional pain points and validate core functionalities.
